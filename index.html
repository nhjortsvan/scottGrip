Scott's grip has similarities with the Western tennis grip; wide separation of the thumb and index finger on the trail (right) hand. 
Hogan's grip, similar to Vardon grip.  He has a note "pressure" where the thumb meets the bottom of the index finger.   The thumb pinches into the index finger.
Scott's grip is very strong. Trail (right) hand has been rotated clockwise (right).  The lead (left) hand is on top of the club, more neutral than the trail hand.  The 'V's of the hands do not line up.
Hogan's iron grip is a neutral.  The 'V's point to the chin.  A driver grip (strong grip) has the 'V's pointing at the right shoulder.   The left and right hand  'V's always align in the same direction.
